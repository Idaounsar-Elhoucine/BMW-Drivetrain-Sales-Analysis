üìä Project Overview
This project analyzes BMW sales data to understand the performance and trends of different vehicle drivetrains over a 15-year period. The core focus is to answer crucial business questions such as:

Which drivetrain generates the highest total sales revenue?

How have the Electric and Hybrid sales grown compared to traditional Petrol and Diesel?

What are the highest and lowest price points across different vehicle types?

Are there dominant color preferences or transmission types for specific drivetrains?

The dashboard is designed for quick, interactive comparison across the four major drivetrain categories.

üí∞ Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
The dashboard prominently displays three key metrics for the selected drivetrain:

Total Sales (in Millions): Revealing the overall revenue generated.

Highest Price: Identifying the most expensive model sold in that category (e.g., $120K for Electric).

Lowest Price: Showing the entry-level price point (e.g., $30K for Diesel).

üìà Sales Trends & Distribution

Sales by Color (Bar Chart): Highlights the most popular vehicle colors for a specific fuel type, indicating clear consumer preferences (e.g., Red and White are top for Petrol).

Sales by Transmission (Donut Chart): Shows the sales distribution between different transmission types (Manual/Automatic) with clear percentage splits.

Car Price By Model & Year (Bar Chart): Tracks how the average price of cars in the selected category has trended over the years.

üß† Key Insights Uncovered
By easily toggling between the four views, the dashboard highlights several critical findings:

Revenue Leadership: Hybrid sales currently lead in total revenue at $64.53M, closely followed by Petrol and Electric.

Price Consistency: The Highest Price point remains stable at around $120K across most drivetrains, suggesting similar high-end model positioning.

Drivetrain Preference: The Electric and Hybrid categories show sales totals that are highly competitive with, or even surpassing, the traditional Diesel and Petrol categories, signaling a rapid market shift.

Transmission Dominance: Across all four views, the 50.1-51.0% segment consistently dominates the Sales by Transmission chart, indicating a strong preference for one specific transmission type.
